Daemon: Steps

To become a daemon, a process must do the following:

  1. Call 
    fork()
    – this creates a new process.
  2. In the parent, call 
    exit()
    – so that init, the great-grandparent, recognizes that the parent of the child is no longer running, and that the child is not a process group leader.
  3. Call 
    setsid()
    – this gives the child a new process group and session, both of which it leads. This ensures the process has no associated controlling terminal.
  4. Change the working directory to the root directory (/) by calling the 
    chdir()
    function.
  5. Close all 
    fd
    s – you don't want to keep any files open anymore, including the keyboard and the screen!.
  6. Open 
    fd
    s 0, 1, and 2 and redirect the input & output of all of them to /dev/null.
