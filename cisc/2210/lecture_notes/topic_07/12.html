Basic Concepts

A few more rules can be inferred from the three axioms:

  1. \( P(\emptyset) = 0 \)
  2. \( P(A^c) = 1 - P(A) \) [$A^c$ is the complement of $A$.]
  3. If $A \subseteq B$, then $P(A) \le P(B)$

Examples:

  • The probability of rolling a $7$ on a die is $0$ since rolling a $7$ is a non-existent (impossible) outcome.
  • The probability of rolling anything but $4$ on a die is $\frac{5}{6}$ since rolling a $4$ has $P[\text{rolling a 4}] = \frac{1}{6}$, so $P[\text{not rolling a 4}]\;=$$\;1 - P[\text{rolling a 4}]\;=$$\;1 - \frac{1}{6}\;=$$\;\frac{5}{6}$.
  • The event of "rolling an even integer" is a subset of the event "rolling a number bigger than $1$" (why so?). Indeed, we have $P[\text{rolling }$$\text{an even }$$\text{integer}]\;=$$\;\frac{3}{6}\;=$$\;\frac{1}{2}$ and $P[\text{rolling }$$\text{a number }$$\text{bigger than }$$\text{1}]\;=$$\;\frac{5}{6}$, and that $\frac{3}{6} < \frac{5}{6}$.
